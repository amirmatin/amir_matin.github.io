Video compressive sensing

Exploring the temploral redundancy of the video frames and achive higher frame rate video acquisition systems

Abstract_This paper investigates a particular application of compressive sensing (CS) for reconstruction of video data cubes and focuses on exploring the after acquisition reconstruction process and propose a new implementation of weighted 3D total variation algorithm with updating parameters for video CS applications. This method enables a fast data reconstruction with higher resolution compared to the other bench mark methods. An optical setup consisting of a standard CCD detector, a high speed Digital Micromirror Device (DMD) with the capability of uniquely encoding each video frames at the given detector acquisition rate and a standard optical beam splitters and tube lenses will enable a high speed data acquisition. Proposed 3D TV method is embedded as a regulariser in an algorithm constructed under the Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers (ADMM) framework. We also discuss the use of wavelets denoising techniques using soft thresholding and compare it to weighted 3D TV with updating parameters. We explore the reconstruction quality by comparing the obtained PSNR values to mentioned existing methods and also discuss the calculation time needed to process the data. Proposed scheme has been simulated on a 2000 frames data (150x150) which is has never been achieved before.
